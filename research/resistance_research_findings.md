## Detailed Organization Profiles

### Tibetan Resistance Movement
- **Students for a Free Tibet (SFT)**
  - Website: https://studentsforafreetibet.org/
  - Structure: Global grassroots network with chapter-based organization
  - Focus: Solidarity with Tibetan people for freedom and independence
  - Methods: Non-violent protests, lobbying, awareness campaigns
  - Social Media: 100K+ Facebook followers, active Twitter presence
  - Partnerships: Works with Asia Freedom Institute and other organizations

### Uyghur Resistance Movement
- **World Uyghur Congress (WUC)**
  - Website: https://www.uyghurcongress.org/en/
  - Structure: International umbrella organization of exiled Uyghur groups
  - Leadership: Represents collective interests of Uyghur people
  - Recognition: 2019 Democracy Award recipient from National Endowment for Democracy
  - Focus: Democracy, human rights, freedom for Uyghurs in East Turkistan
  - Methods: Peaceful, nonviolent, democratic advocacy
  - Current Issues: Documenting ongoing harassment by CCP in Xinjiang

### Falun Gong Resistance Network
- **Persecution Documentation**: Comprehensive campaign since 1999
- **Status**: Regarded as "evil cult" by Chinese government, banned
- **Global Reach**: Transnational repression targeting practitioners worldwide
- **Documentation**: Freedom House reports on systematic persecution
- **Resistance Methods**: Information dissemination, legal advocacy, awareness campaigns

## London Mega Embassy Opposition

### Current Status (December 2024)
- **Decision Delayed**: UK government postponed decision until January 2025
- **Location**: 5.5-acre complex near Tower Bridge, would be largest diplomatic outpost
- **Opposition Concerns**:
  - Enhanced espionage capabilities in heart of London
  - Transnational repression operations hub
  - National security risks
  - Surveillance and intelligence gathering

### Key Opposition Arguments
- **Security Risks**: Potential espionage hub in central London
- **Size Concerns**: Massive 5.5-acre complex unprecedented for diplomatic mission
- **Timing**: Decision aligned with broader UK-China relations tensions
- **International Precedent**: Could encourage similar mega-embassies globally

## Chinese Dissidents in Exile

### Major Organizations and Networks
- **Chinese Alliance for Democracy (CAD)**: First overseas political opposition since 1949
- **Overseas Opposition Movement (OOM)**: Broader network of exile organizations
- **Academic Networks**: University-based dissident communities

### Transnational Repression Targeting
- **Scope**: China conducts most sophisticated global repression campaign
- **Methods**: Using Interpol to pursue dissidents, businesspeople, Uyghur rights activists
- **Documentation**: Freedom House identifies China as top perpetrator
- **Target Lists**: Leaked documents show "49 Overseas Members of Reactionary Organizations"

## Leaked Information Sources

### Major Document Leaks
- **Xinjiang Papers**: 400+ pages of internal Chinese government documents
  - Source: https://xjdp.aspi.org.au/explainers/the-leaked-documents/
  - Content: Government manual for operating Uyghur detention camps
  - Impact: First published evidence of systematic detention policies

- **Karakax Document**: Detailed surveillance reports on Uyghur families
  - Source: CNN investigation (2020)
  - Content: Beijing's justification for mass detentions
  - Significance: Reveals individual-level surveillance and control

- **CCP Global Crackdown Files**: Recent leak exposing worldwide dissent suppression
  - Date: February 2025
  - Content: CCP efforts to "suppress Falun Gong's momentum" globally
  - Scope: Operations in United States and other countries
