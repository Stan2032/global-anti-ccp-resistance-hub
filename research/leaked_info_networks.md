# Leaked Information Sources and Underground Networks

## Major Investigative Organizations

### International Consortium of Investigative Journalists (ICIJ)
- **Website**: https://www.icij.org/
- **Key Investigations**:
  - **China Cables** (2019): Operating manuals for Xinjiang detention camps
  - **China Targets** (2025): Transnational repression investigation
  - **Offshore Holdings**: Leaked records of Chinese elite financial activities
- **Network**: 190+ investigative journalists across 65+ countries
- **Document Sources**: Chain of exiled Uyghurs, whistleblowers, leaked government files
- **Authentication**: Documents verified by multiple independent sources
- **Impact**: Exposed mass detention operations, surveillance systems, repression tactics

### Australian Strategic Policy Institute (ASPI)
- **Xinjiang Data Project**: https://xjdp.aspi.org.au/
- **Key Documents**: First published Chinese government manual for operating camps
- **Focus**: Systematic documentation of Uyghur persecution
- **Methods**: Analysis of leaked government documents, satellite imagery, witness testimony
- **Partnerships**: International media collaborations, academic institutions

## Major Document Leaks

### China Cables (2019)
- **Source**: Chain of exiled Uyghurs to ICIJ
- **Content**: Secret Chinese government documents on Xinjiang operations
- **Scope**: Operating manuals for mass detention camps
- **Authentication**: Confirmed by multiple independent experts
- **Impact**: First concrete evidence of systematic detention policies
- **Global Reach**: Published by 17 media partners worldwide

### Xinjiang Papers (2019)
- **Source**: New York Times investigation
- **Content**: 400+ pages of internal Chinese documents
- **Key Revelations**: Xi Jinping speeches calling for "absolutely no mercy"
- **Scope**: Security, population control, punishment strategies
- **Timeline**: Documents from April 2014 onwards
- **Significance**: Direct evidence of top-level policy decisions

### Karakax Document (2020)
- **Source**: CNN investigation
- **Content**: Detailed surveillance reports on individual Uyghur families
- **Scope**: Beijing's justification for mass detentions
- **Methods**: Individual-level surveillance and control documentation
- **Impact**: Revealed personal targeting and family separation policies

### Great Firewall Leak (2025)
- **Source**: The Epoch Times investigation
- **Content**: 193 developers identified in censorship system
- **Scope**: Technical infrastructure of internet censorship
- **Significance**: Exposed personnel and methods behind information control

### CCP Global Crackdown Files (2025)
- **Source**: Multiple investigative outlets
- **Content**: Documents revealing worldwide dissent suppression
- **Scope**: Operations targeting Falun Gong practitioners globally
- **Geographic Reach**: United States and other countries
- **Timeline**: Recent leak from February 2025

## Whistleblower Protection and Channels

### Current Challenges in China
- **No Statutory Protection**: Chinese labor law provides no whistleblower protection
